Bench Press

The Bench press exercise is done by holding a weighted barbell to your chest while lying on a bench and then extending your arms until your elbows are locked. It is very easy to perform all you have to do is lay down on flat bench and after that get one rode with weight plates and lift up with your arms. Make sure don't try heavy weights if you are a beginner.

Rowing Machine

The rowing machine is very good for your heart and lungs. It also build your legs muscles, arms and upper back. Rowing machines also build your stamina and burn calories. Simply just sit down on machine and put your legs on the front foot pads and then pull the rope.

Treadmill

The treadmill is very handy in your daily exercise routine and is an easy way to get your cardio in. All you have to do is run on the moving belt, you can control your running speed by adjusting the speed and incline using the control buttons. Running helps you to lose weight and build stamina.

Chest-machine

The chest machine is similar to using the bench press, but with the chest machine you have to sit down and pull two small bars using your arms towards your chest. This exercise helps build muscle and is used only to work out your chest and back.

Dumbbells

Dumbbells are a great way to build your shoulders, triceps, biceps and chest muscles. You can use dumbbells for your chest, just lay down on the bench and lift up your arms. For triceps and biceps just stand up, lift up the dumbbells and fold your arms and open your arms.

Battle Rope

The battle Rope exercise is for your upper and lower body. If you do hard rope waves then you can burn 50 calories in just 5 minutes. You can wave the rope up and down as well as left and right. It provides a good workout to your legs, arms and feet.
